About

About

I am a doctoral student at the School of Education, Tsinghua University, specializing in the study of parenting, adolescent psychology, and AI-empowered family education. My research sits at the intersection of educational psychology, developmental science, and emerging technology — asking, at its core: how do the people around a child shape who that child becomes?

My curiosity began early. Growing up with a background in early childhood education and working as a volunteer teacher in rural areas of China, I became fascinated not just by children's development, but by the adults in their orbit — parents, teachers, communities — and how those relationships ripple into a child's learning and wellbeing.

I sometimes think of my ideal research contribution as a kind of Doraemon pocket: not a magic fix, but a quietly intelligent support system that is always there for families and children when they need it most.

That question has guided my work ever since. I am interested in connecting with researchers and practitioners working on parenting, family education technology, and human-AI interaction in educational contexts.

Empirical Research

Skilled in mixed-methods (quantitative + qualitative) research. Led and contributed to over ten research projects with expertise in data analysis and survey design.

Human-AI Interaction

Investigating how AI mediates family education and child development, and how parents and children interact with intelligent systems.

Global Perspective

Presented at AERA, SRCD, EECERA, and AIED. Experienced in cross-cultural comparative research with international academic networks.

Teaching Practice

Served as teaching assistant for Educational Psychology, Academic Writing, and Introduction to Family Education. Independently led discussion sections and guided student papers.
